Welcome to the Deci Research team at NVIDIA, led by Prof. Ran El‑Yaniv.

Founded in 2020 within Deci AI (acquired by NVIDIA in 2024), we operate as part of the Deci group, led by Dr. Yonatan Geifman.

Our mission is to improve the inference efficiency of deep neural networks. We leverage Neural Architecture Search (NAS) to reduce latency and increase throughput, enabling more cost‑effective deployment of state‑of‑the‑art models.

Puzzle: Distillation-Based NAS for Inference-Optimized LLMs

ICML, 2025

TL;DR: Puzzle accelerates LLM inference on specific hardware using blockwise local knowledge distillation and MIP, preserving quality while reducing cost.

Paper Video Code Read more

Despite LLMs’ impressive results, inference cost is a bottleneck. Puzzle optimizes large-scale models for target hardware without sacrificing accuracy, achieving up to 2.17× speedups.

Authors: Akhiad Bercovich, Tomer Ronen, Talor Abramovich, Nir Ailon, Nave Assaf, Mohammad Dabbah, Ido Galil, Amnon Geifman, Yonatan Geifman, Izhak Golan, Netanel Haber, Ehud Karpas, Roi Koren, Itay Levy, Pavlo Molchanov, Shahar Mor, Zach Moshe, Najeeb Nabwani, Omri Puny, Ran Rubin, Itamar Schen, Ido Shahaf, Oren Tropp, Omer Ullman Argov, Ran Zilberstein, Ran El‑Yaniv

Llama‑Nemotron: Efficient Reasoning Models

ICML, 2025 — EXAIT Workshop

TL;DR: Open reasoning LLMs (8B/49B/253B) matching SOTA reasoning quality while improving throughput and memory; dynamic reasoning toggle for controllable compute.

Paper Read more

Heterogeneous reasoning models trained for both quality and efficiency: architecture search for faster inference, distillation, continued pretraining, and reasoning‑focused post‑training (SFT + large‑scale RL). The family achieves competitive reasoning with higher throughput and memory efficiency and supports switching between standard chat and reasoning modes.

Authors (abridged): Akhiad Bercovich, Itay Levy, Izik Golan, Mohammad Dabbah, Ran El‑Yaniv, Omri Puny, Ido Galil, Zach Moshe, Tomer Ronen, Najeeb Nabwani, Ido Shahaf, Oren Tropp, Ehud Karpas, Ran Zilberstein, Jiaqi Zeng, Soumye Singhal, Alexander Bukharin, Yian Zhang, Tugrul Konuk, Gerald Shen, Ameya Sunil Mahabaleshwarkar, Bilal Kartal, Yoshi Suhara, Olivier Delalleau, Zijia Chen, Zhilin Wang, David Mosallanezhad, Adi Renduchintala, Haifeng Qian, Dima Rekesh, …

FFN Fusion: Rethinking Sequential Computation in Large Language Models

NeurIPS, 2025 (Spotlight)

TL;DR: Fuse consecutive FFN layers into larger blocks to reduce sequential depth and accelerate inference with minimal accuracy impact.

Paper Read more

Puzzle discovers hardware‑aware designs; fusing FFNs decreases depth. Across tens to hundreds of billions of parameters, FFN Fusion reduces latency and cost while preserving quality, complementing quantization and pruning.

Authors: Akhiad Bercovich, Mohammad Dabbah, Omri Puny, Ido Galil, Amnon Geifman, Yonatan Geifman, Izhak Golan, Ehud Karpas, Itay Levy, Zach Moshe, Najeeb Nabwani, Tomer Ronen, Itamar Schen, Elad Segal, Ido Shahaf, Oren Tropp, Ran Zilberstein, Ran El‑Yaniv
